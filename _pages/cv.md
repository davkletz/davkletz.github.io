## Research Interests

My research is situated at the intersection of theoretical linguistics and natural language processing, within the field of computational linguistics. I approach this discipline as a branch of language sciences, where computational methods serve to interrogate and illuminate linguistic theories.

**Primary Research Line:** Understanding how linguistic theory can inform and improve neural language model architectures and training procedures.

**Current Research Topics:**
- Negation processing in pretrained language models
- Hallucination detection and mitigation in RAG systems
- Multilingual capabilities and language family influences in LLMs
- Model interpretability and compositional semantics
- Distributional semantics and contextual embeddings

**Methodological Approaches:**
- Formal semantics and compositionality
- Distributional semantics and vector representations
- Syntax-semantics interface
- Language model evaluation and probing
